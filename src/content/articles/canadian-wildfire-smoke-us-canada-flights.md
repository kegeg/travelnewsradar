Canadian wildfire smoke has been pushing into parts of the US Midwest, Great Lakes, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic through mid-July 2026, and it is still a summer travel problem worth planning for. Low visibility from smoke means controllers space planes out more, so flights take longer to get in and out. That shows up as cascading delays, missed connections, and the occasional cancellation on Canada-US routes through hubs like Toronto, Chicago, Detroit, and the New York-area airports.

Canada’s [July wildfire-season update](https://www.canada.ca/en/public-safety-canada/news/2026/07/the-government-of-canada-provides-an-update-regarding-the-2026-wildfire-season--july-update.html) still counted hundreds of active fires nationally, with elevated fire danger expected to continue in parts of the North and into northern Ontario and Quebec through August. Smoke often drifts far from the fires themselves into the US.

## What this means for travelers

If you are flying through or into smoke-hit regions in the coming weeks, check your flight the same way you would for bad weather: look before you leave for the airport, and give yourself more connection time.

Practical checks:

1. Check whether **your flight** is delayed or canceled on the day of travel (airline app, email/SMS alerts, or the airline site).
2. Check the [FAA National Airspace System status](https://nasstatus.faa.gov/) for ground delays and airport restrictions that may not show up in the app right away.
3. Check air quality where you are landing or connecting ([US AirNow](https://www.airnow.gov/) / [Canada’s AQHI](https://www.canada.ca/en/environment-climate-change/services/air-quality-health-index.html)). Smoke is also an outdoor-travel issue, not only a runway issue.
4. Look for an airline **travel waiver** before you pay change fees. Carriers sometimes open short fee-free rebooking windows during smoke waves; those windows close fast and are not automatic.
5. Assume smoke-related slowdowns are treated like **weather**. That usually means no airline cash compensation for a simple delay. A cancellation or major schedule change is a different conversation (refund / rebooking rules).
6. If you have a tight connection through a Midwest or Northeast hub, give yourself more time than usual, or prefer a nonstop when the fare is close.

## What to do if your flight is delayed or canceled

Same things as when there are weather issues, or other disruptions:

- Turn on airline alerts early.
- Search your own rebooking options instead of waiting for the first automatic offer. Make the customer service life easy and it's a lot more likely you'll get what you want.
- Before you pay out of pocket for a hotel or meals, know whether **flight delay insurance** on the card that paid for the ticket covers you.

That last point is important and is one a lot of travelers skip unknowingly. If you paid for the flight with a credit card that includes flight delay insurance (true for most travel credit cards in both Canada and the US), you can often get hotel and meal costs reimbursed after a long enough delay, and that can still apply when the cause is weather or smoke. Thresholds and caps vary by card, so check your benefits guide once, before you need it.

For more detail, see [the most important things to know before a disruption](https://flytrippers.com/most-important-things-related-to-canceled-and-delayed-flights/) and [what to do when the delay is already happening](https://flytrippers.com/6-things-to-do-when-your-flight-is-canceled-or-delayed/). The short version: always book your flights on a card with delay coverage, keep receipts, and do not assume the airline hotel voucher is your only option.

Also check for a waiver before you pay to make changes to your booking. Waivers are temporary airline policies that drop change fees (and sometimes fare differences) so people can move off overloaded days. They are common in disruption weeks and easy to miss if you only look at the app’s change-fee quote.

## Why delay insurance is worth caring about

One example: we missed a connection in Warsaw a few years back after a delay, booked the airport Marriott ourselves, went to sleep, and flew out the next day. The stay was expensive on the night, so we earned even more hotel points on it and the flight delay insurance covered it all. That pattern has worked for us many times, claim the hotel and meals, move on. This is why paying with a card that has flight delay insurance is one of the best basic and easy tips for everyone.

## Bottom line

Canadian wildfire smoke is still in the summer travel picture for parts of the US and Canada. Before you fly through the Midwest, Northeast, or major Canadian hubs, check FAA status, airline alerts, and air quality. If you get stuck overnight, receipts plus card delay insurance beat standing in line hoping for a voucher. And if an airline posts a waiver, use it before you pay change fees.
