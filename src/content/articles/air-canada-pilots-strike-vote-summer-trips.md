Air Canada pilots have authorized strike action, raising the odds of disrupted flights for travelers using Canada’s largest airline this summer. If you hold tickets in the coming weeks, treat this as a planning problem, not a rumor to ignore.

The vote does not mean planes stop tomorrow. It does mean the airline and union enter a sharper phase of talks, and last-minute schedule changes become more likely if negotiations stall.

## What this means for travelers

If you are booked on Air Canada (or a codeshare metal operated by Air Canada) for peak summer dates, build a backup plan now. Same-day surprises are harder to fix when every seat is full.

Nomads and multi-city travelers should watch connection banks through Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver especially closely — those hubs amplify delays when crew availability tightens.

## Who is most affected

- Travelers with nonstop or connecting Air Canada flights in the next 2–6 weeks
- People with tight international connections through Canadian hubs
- Award bookings (less flexibility if you need to rebook quickly)

## What to do now

1. Screenshot your itinerary and check the app for schedule changes daily.
2. Know your rights and rebooking options before you need them.
3. If your trip is immovable (wedding, cruise embarkation), compare a paid change now versus waiting.
